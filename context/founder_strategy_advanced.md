# Advanced Founder Strategy: Conflict & Resolution

## 1. Founder Vesting & Breakups
- **Rule**: 4-year vesting with a 1-year cliff is non-negotiable. If a founder leaves before 12 months, they get 0%.
- **Resolution**: If a founder breakup is inevitable, do it early. A 'clean break' at $100k ARR is 100x better than a lawsuit at $10M ARR.
- **Tactic**: Use 'Shotgun Clauses' in your shareholder agreement to handle irreconcilable deadlocks.

## 2. Hiring the First 10 (The 'A-Team')
- **Filter**: "Would I work for this person?" If the answer is no, don't hire them.
- **Compensation**: High equity, low salary. You want missionaries, not mercenaries.
- **Firing**: Fire fast. A 'B-Player' in the first 10 will attract other B-Players and kill your culture.

## 3. Product Development Traps
- **The Pivot**: Don't pivot because things are 'hard'. Pivot because you've found a 'pull' from a different market that you can't ignore.
- **Fake Work**: Writing blog posts, attending conferences, and 'networking' are fake work. Real work is writing code and talking to users.
- **Consulting Trap**: Never trade equity for 'consulting services'. If they believe in the company, they should invest or work for equity.

## 4. Market Selection (The 'Sequoia' Filter)
- **Small Markets**: Don't build for a small market unless it's growing at 100% YoY.
- **Incumbent Weakness**: Target markets where the incumbent is a 'legacy' player (e.g., banks, old HR software) and the user experience is universally hated.
- **Alpha**: The best companies solve a problem the founder personally experienced.
