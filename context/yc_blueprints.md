# Y Combinator Growth Blueprints & Tactics

## 1. The Growth-First Mindset
Growth is the only metric that matters in the early stages. If you aren't growing at 5-7% weekly, you don't have product-market fit.
- **Tactic**: Set a weekly growth goal. If you miss it, treat it as a hair-on-fire emergency.
- **Blueprint**: "Do things that don't scale" (Paul Graham). Manually recruit your first 100 users.

## 2. Retention is the Foundation
Growth without retention is a leaky bucket.
- **Metric**: The 'N-Day Retention' curve should flatten out above zero. If it hits zero, your product doesn't provide enough value.
- **Tactic**: Talk to users who churned. Ask "Why did you stop?" and "What would make you come back?"

## 3. High-Leverage Fundraising (YC Style)
- **The Rule of 10**: If you've had 10 meetings and no check, something is wrong with your pitch or your market.
- **The Handshake Protocol**: Never leave a meeting without a clear "Yes" or "No". "Let me think about it" is a No.
- **Safe Notes**: Always use standard post-money Safes to avoid legal friction.

## 4. Engineering for Speed
- **The 2-Week Launch Rule**: If your MVP takes more than 2 weeks to build, you are over-engineering.
- **Tactic**: Cut features ruthlessly. Your MVP should do exactly one thing exceptionally well.
- **Blueprint**: Use battle-tested tech stacks (PostgreSQL, Node.js, React). Don't experiment with your infrastructure during your first 6 months.

## 5. Sales & Distribution Gaps
- **Target**: Enterprise SaaS.
- **Alpha**: The "middle manager" is the best entry point. They have budget but less oversight than executives.
- **Tactic**: Offer a 30-day pilot. Make it impossible for them to say no by removing all integration friction.
